Why Inflation is a Scam Designed to Tax Your Time

Published March 28, 2026 · Digital Rebel

Every year, the purchasing power of your savings quietly evaporates. Not because of bad luck — but because of a deliberate architectural feature of the modern monetary system. This is not a conspiracy. It is policy.

The Mechanism

Central banks target 2% annual inflation as a feature, not a bug. This means in 35 years, your dollar is worth half what it is today. The laborer who saves in fiat is systematically taxed without a vote, without a line item, without a name.

Every dollar saved is a loan to the system — repaid in devalued currency.

Who Benefits?

Asset holders. When money supply expands, value flows first to those who receive new money — governments, financial institutions, and the asset-rich. By the time it reaches wage earners, prices have adjusted. This is the Cantillon Effect.

The Exit

The exit is not rage. The exit is understanding. Hard assets, productive equity, scarce digital commodities — these preserve labor over time. The goal is not to beat inflation. The goal is to own things inflation cannot touch.
